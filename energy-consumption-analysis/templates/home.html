Analytics Sections

Click any section below to expand it and explore the analysis.

1. Electricity Consumption Over Time ▼

This section visualizes electricity consumption over time to observe overall demand patterns and long-term trends across all monitored states.

Analytical Insight

  • The data illustrates a steady increase in consumption moving across the time interval observed.
  • Notable spikes are visible at key intervals, correlating to likely demand surges or industrial activity.
  • Overall trend shows a consistent upward growth in baseload electricity requirements.
2. State Wise Electricity Consumption ▼

A comparison of average electricity consumption across different states or regions to understand geographic demand distribution.

Analytical Insight

  • Maharashtra consistently records the highest average consumption driven by its industrial and population scale.
  • Karnataka shows the lowest average, though it registered isolated high anomaly spikes.
  • Differences arise from varying industrial output, urbanization rates, and climate cooling demands per region.
3. Monthly Energy Consumption Trend ▼

Aggregate electricity usage grouped by month to surface macro-level seasonal demand patterns across all states.

Analytical Insight

  • Monthly consumption shows a clear rising pattern as the year progresses through the tracked period.
  • Higher electricity demand months align with warmer temperatures, increasing cooling appliance usage.
  • Seasonal industrial ramping phases also contribute to the cumulative month-over-month rise.
4. Peak Load Period Analysis ▼

Identifies the specific dates on which combined electricity demand across all regions reached its absolute maximum level.

Analytical Insight

  • The highlighted red point captures the day of maximum concurrent energy draw from the grid.
  • Recognizing peak intervals allows energy providers to schedule peaker plants and prevent blackouts.
  • Proactive peak management reduces overload risks and optimizes load balancing across the grid.
5. Anomaly Detection (Abnormal Spikes) ▼

Uses machine learning (IsolationForest) to flag data points that deviate significantly from normal consumption behavior.

Analytical Insight

  • Red points indicate statistically abnormal electricity draws that fall outside expected bounds.
  • Possible causes include extreme weather events, sudden industrial surges, or equipment faults.
  • Early anomaly identification allows grid operators to investigate causes and improve resilience.
